WHAT IS DA BRAIDR?

Da Braidr is a multimodal research engine developed to critically examine the impact of Generative AI on Black braiding communities. A central question driving this inquiry is: How might generative AI reconfigure braiding experiences that have historically relied on deeply embodied, interpersonal, and communal practices passed through generations? What is transformed when these practices—grounded in care, community, and artistry—are mediated by technology? From the process of identifying a braided hairstyle to selecting the expert who brings it to life, Da Braidr seeks to understand how AI could reshape these Black cultural practices. Begin by describing your imagined hairstyle in a text prompt. Da Braidr will translate your description into a visual representation, alongside an analysis of the braid pattern, and structure. Finally, text Da Braidr to connect you with an expert who will realize your AI-generated braids.



This art project, led by Minne Atairu builds on existing hair-related Generative AI projects: Fro Studies (2022), Box Braids Study (2022), Blonde Braids Study (2022), Cornrow Studies (2022), Ribbon Studies (2022), Beaded Braids Studies (2022).

TECHNICAL DESCRIPTION

Da Braidr leverages the vision-language capabilities of OpenAI models, fine-tuned specifically for the task of braid pattern recognition. The system's vision model is fine-tuned using open-source datasets that prominently feature braids on Black folks. To enrich the dataset, I have created a portal to receive human-contributed, real-world image data. I do not currently accept synthetic data. All image submissions are analyzed to verify their origin.

OPT-IN FOR BRAID DATA

Da Braidr's image generation system relies on real-world image data to capture and reproduce the richness of Black braided hairstyles. By contributing your image data, you'll help optimize Da Braidr’s ability to understand and replicate key ethnomathematical concepts that braiders intuitively employ such as rotation, tessalation, translation, reflection and dilation. These mathematical principles underpin critical aspects of braiding-the spacing between braids (translation), the geometric sectioning of hair (tessellation), the orientation/curvature of braids (rotation), symmetry in braided patterns (reflection), the expansion/contraction points in a braid structure (dilation). Through your image contributions, I will optimize Da Braidr's understanding of these principles to ensure that the system aligns with evolving braided hairstyles and their associated mathematical principles.

Please review the following image submission guidelines:

  1. Clear, high-quality images are preferred to ensure the hairstyle details are well-captured.
  2. Include different angles (front, back, sides) to provide a complete view of the style.
  3. Only share images that you have the right to distribute (i.e., your own hairstyles or hairstyles that you have permission to share).
