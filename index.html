Da Braidr is a multimodal research engine designed to explore how Generative AI may influence the evolution of Black braiding communities. It enables tasks ranging from generating specific braid styles to connecting users with professional hair braiders. The system's vision model is fine-tuned using diverse, open-source datasets that prominently feature braiding styles. We have also established a dedicated portal to receive user-contributed image data, currently accepting only non-synthetic images to maintain authenticity and quality in representation.

This project by Minne Atairu builds on existing hair-related Generative AI projects: Fro Studies (2022), Box Braids Study (2022), Blonde Braids Study (2022), Cornrow Studies (2022), Ribbon Studies (2022), Beaded Braids Studies (2022).

TECHNICAL DESCRIPTION
Da Braidr leverages the vision-language capabilities of OpenAI models, fine-tuned specifically for the task of braid pattern recognition
