WHAT IS DA BRAIDR?

Da Braidr is a multimodal research engine developed to examine the impact of Generative AI on Black hair braiding communities. This includes the cultural practices and traditions that shape the design and execution of natural/synthetic braided hairstyles worn by people of African descent. Central to this inquiry is the question: How might generative AI reconfigure hair braiding experiences that have historically been passed down through embodied, interpersonal, and community-driven exchanges across generations? From the identification of specific braided styles to the selection of a skilled braider, what is fundamentally transformed when these culturally rooted practices are mediated through a multimodal generative system?

I chose multimodality as a framework for this research engine because it offers a means to simulate, rather than abstract, Black braiding practices. The image generation system allows you to describe and visualize your envisioned braid. The image recognition system analysez and provides you with a description of the pattern, and structure. The conversational system responds to inquiries you might have about the generated braids and suggests potential hair care products to help you achieve the simulated style.

This art project, led by Minne Atairu builds on existing hair-related Generative AI projects: Fro Studies (2022), Box Braids Study (2022), Blonde Braids Study (2022), Cornrow Studies (2022), Ribbon Studies (2022), Beaded Braids Studies (2022).

TECHNICAL DESCRIPTION

Da Braidr leverages the vision-language capabilities of OpenAI models, fine-tuned specifically for the task of braid pattern recognition. The system's vision model is fine-tuned using open-source datasets that prominently feature braids on Black folks. To enrich the dataset, I have created a portal to receive human-contributed, real-world image data. I do not currently accept synthetic data. All image submissions are analyzed to verify their origin.

OPT-IN FOR BRAID DATA

Da Braidr's image generation system relies on real-world image data to capture and reproduce the richness of Black braided hairstyles. By contributing your image data, you'll help optimize Da Braidrâ€™s ability to understand and replicate key ethnomathematical concepts that braiders intuitively employ such as rotation, tessalation, translation, reflection and dilation. These mathematical principles underpin critical aspects of braiding-the spacing between braids (translation), the geometric sectioning of hair (tessellation), the orientation/curvature of braids (rotation), symmetry in braided patterns (reflection), the expansion/contraction points in a braid structure (dilation). Through your image contributions, I will optimize Da Braidr's understanding of these principles to ensure that the system aligns with evolving braided hairstyles and their associated mathematical principles.

Please review the following image submission guidelines:

  1. Clear, high-quality images are preferred to ensure the hairstyle details are well-captured.
  2. Include different angles (front, back, sides) to provide a complete view of the style.
  3. Only share images that you have the right to distribute (i.e., your own hairstyles or hairstyles that you have permission to share).
